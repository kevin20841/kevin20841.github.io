# home

I am a composer, pianist, music scholar, and (semi-retired) computer scientist. Compositionally, I am interested in ways of questioning, unsettling, and destabilizing "universal" aesthetic notions about meaning and listening considered stable in experimental art music. I am also intrigued by notions of legibility/illegibility, and making music that signifies differently to different people.
<br>
## research interests

- the role of international political institutions in shaping modernist aesthetics
- moving beyond intercultural framings of art music towards a more textured understanding of their postcolonial nature
- how musicians in new music contribute to, negotiate with, and contest the formation of Han identity both within and outside China

<br>

## some books I am currently reading 
- _Wild Sound: Maryanne Amacher and the Tenses of Audible Life_, by Amy Cimini
- _White Musical Mythologies: Sonic Presence in Modernism_, by Edmund Mendelssohn
- _Musical Migration and Imperial New York: Early Cold War Scenes_, by Brigid Cohen
- _Coming to Terms with the Nation: Ethnic Classification in Modern China_ by Thomas S. Mullaney
